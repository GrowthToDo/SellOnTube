## 3. Competitor Landscape

### 3A. Competitor Blog Analysis

#### YouTubeRankTracker.com

**Post 1:** "How to Find What Keywords My YouTube Videos Rank For"
- ~2,200 words. Outlines YouTube Studio limitations, then positions their paid tool as the only solution.
- Angle: Tool-promotion with creator framing. 8+ product mentions.
- Gaps: No ROI framework, no connection to revenue, no guidance for teams or agencies, no cost-benefit analysis.

**Post 2:** "How to See All Keywords a YouTube Channel Ranks For"
- ~2,100 words. Same pattern: Studio is broken, our tool fixes it.
- Angle: Creator-focused, hard sell.
- Gaps: No business metrics integration, no attribution modeling, no implementation timeline expectations.

#### Arvow (arvow.com)

**Post:** "What Keywords Does Your YouTube Channel Actually Rank For?"
- ~3,200 words. The longest and most educational of the three. Uses an Ahrefs analogy to explain the concept. Includes a real data example.
- Angle: Creator-focused education leading to tool promotion. 10+ YouTubeRankTracker mentions plus Arvow AI upsells.
- Gaps: No ROI connection, no business prioritization framework, assumes the reader's goal is views/growth, not lead generation.

#### Reliqus

**Post:** "YouTube Rank Tracking Tools in 2026"
- ~1,800 words. Covers TubeBuddy, vidIQ, Ahrefs, Morningfame.
- Angle: Neutral-to-business. The closest to useful for a business reader, but still lists tools without evaluation criteria beyond features.
- Gaps: No pricing comparison table, no ROI metrics, no guidance on choosing based on business size or goals.

#### Geekflare

**Post:** "9 YouTube Rank Checker Tools for Streamers and Influencers"
- Lists TubeBuddy, Semrush, Nightwatch, TubeRanker, AccuRanker, YTRank, GeoRanker, vidIQ, YouTubeRank.
- Angle: Creator/influencer focused. Title says "Streamers and Influencers."
- Gaps: No business-owner lens. No evaluation of which tools help with lead gen vs view growth.

Every competitor blog answers: "How do I find/track my YouTube rankings?"

None answer: "What do I do with this ranking data to generate more leads for my business?"

This is the content gap SellonTube fills. The business-owner playbook (what to do with ranking data once you have it) is entirely uncontested.

**Strengths:**
- Free with no paid tier (cleanest free tool in the category)
- Business-owner framing (unique in category)
- Shows competitor landscape data (top 10 with view counts + age)
- No login required for first 3 checks
- Email gate captures business emails specifically (blocks consumer domains)

## 7. Risks & Cautions

1. **The tool page is very new.** Most positions are 50-90+. The amplification strategy is about building topical authority and creating pathways to the tool, not pushing existing rankings. Expect 2-3 months before the blog posts meaningfully impact tool page rankings.

2. **"youtube seo checker" cannibalization risk.** This 14-impression query is hitting the ranking checker page instead of the SEO tool page. The internal linking fix (Quick Win #3) should resolve this, but monitor after implementation.

3. **Post 2 vs existing "Best YouTube SEO Tools" post.** These posts target different primary keywords and different tool sets, but they share vidIQ and TubeBuddy. Ensure distinct H1s, distinct meta descriptions, and cross-link explicitly. If Google conflates them, consider merging later.

4. **No business-framed queries exist yet.** Nobody searches "youtube rank checker for business." The blog posts should use the generic keywords in titles/H1s (matching what people actually search) and frame the answer for business owners in the body copy. Do not optimize titles for "for business" phrasing that nobody searches.

5. **Competitor content is thin but well-established.** YouTubeRankTracker's posts are heavily indexed and likely have domain authority from their tool. SellonTube's edge is depth + business framing, not domain authority. The blog posts need to be genuinely better (more actionable, more specific, better structured) to compete.

6. **Publishing cadence check needed.** Before setting publish dates for both posts, check how many posts fall in the same 7-day window. Max 2 posts/week per CLAUDE.md rules. These two posts should be published at least 5-7 days apart.
