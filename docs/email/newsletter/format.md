# SellonTube Monthly Newsletter Format

The repeatable format for the monthly plain-text newsletter. Contacts graduate here after
finishing the 7-email nurture sequence. Goal: awareness, education, brand-building, word of
mouth. Not a sales channel. Sent as a manual Loops campaign.

---

## Name

Candidates:

1. **The Buyer View** (RECOMMENDED) - one word swap on "viewer" that carries the entire
   SellonTube thesis: buyers, not viewers. Simple, B2B, ownable, works in a subject line.
2. **The Intent Gap** - named after the core concept from our best-performing content
   (views measure reach, leads measure intent). Sharp, but slightly negative as a masthead.
3. **Pipeline Frame** - "how to frame YouTube as pipeline, not media". Clear B2B flavor,
   less distinctive than option 1.

Use **The Buyer View** unless the user overrides.

## Positioning line (reuse in issue 1 intro and the Loops campaign description)

> One YouTube-as-acquisition lesson a month for B2B founders. Numbers, not hype.

---

## Fixed section structure

Every issue has exactly five parts, in this order. Total body: 250-400 words. Plain text,
no images, no HTML formatting beyond what Loops does to plain text.

1. **Lead insight** (~150 words)
   One standalone mini-lesson the reader can use without clicking anything. Must contain at
   least one real number (from a blog post, a case study, or live GSC/client data). Never a
   content roundup intro, never "this month on the blog". Teach one thing. End with a
   one-line takeaway or decision rule.

2. **From the archive** (2-3 lines)
   One blog post pick with full URL (https://sellontube.com/blog/<slug>) and a single
   why-it-matters line. Pick the post that extends the lead insight, or a post that earned
   attention that month (rankings, citations, replies). Not necessarily new.

3. **Tool tip** (2-3 lines)
   One free tool with full URL (https://sellontube.com/tools/<slug>) and one concrete usage
   tip: what to enter, what to look at in the output. Rotate tools across issues; do not
   repeat the same tool two issues in a row.

4. **Forward seed** (1 line)
   A natural variant of "know a founder wrestling with YouTube? forward this to them".
   Rewrite it fresh each issue so it never reads templated. Tie it to the issue's topic
   when possible.

5. **Sign-off**
   ```
   Gautham
   Co-founder, SellonTube
   ```
   No unsubscribe text (Loops appends it). No P.S. sales pitch. The only standing
   commercial line allowed is one light footer sentence above the signature, e.g.
   "We run YouTube end to end for a handful of B2B companies. Reply if you ever want
   to talk about yours." Keep it to one sentence, no link, no urgency.

A themed issue (lead insight, archive pick, and tool all on one topic) is good, not
required. Never force it.

---

## Subject-line convention

Format: `The Buyer View #N: <specific hook>`

- The hook is the lead insight's sharpest number or claim, under ~45 characters after
  the prefix. Examples: `The Buyer View #1: one client pays for the year`,
  `The Buyer View #4: 370 views, 3 customers`.
- Numbers over adjectives. No clickbait, no "you won't believe", no emoji.
- Preview text (if set in Loops): the first line of the body, not a second headline.

## Cadence rules

- Monthly. One issue, no exceptions for "extra" issues.
- Send in the first week of the month, Tuesday to Thursday only. Weekdays only is a hard
  rule; Tue-Thu is the target window.
- Morning IST send is fine; list is small (<1,000) so send-time optimization is not worth
  effort yet.
- If the first week has no Tue-Thu slot free, slip to the second week rather than sending
  Friday or Monday.

## Voice checks (before every send)

- Data-driven, direct, founder-to-founder. Short sentences. B2B acquisition lens only:
  no subscriber-count hype, no creator/influencer framing.
- No em dashes. Grep the draft for the banned list: delve, pivotal, tapestry, landscape,
  showcase, foster, vibrant, intricate, leverage, crucial, vital, unprecedented,
  game-changer, "serves as", "In today's", "it's worth noting", "I hope this resonates".
- No forced rule-of-three, no generic positive ending. End on the forward seed or the
  footer line, not on "here's to a great month".
- Word count 250-400. Count it.

---

## 10-minute assembly checklist

1. (2 min) Pick the lead insight: scan recent posts, GSC pulls, or client conversations
   for one number worth teaching. One number, one lesson.
2. (3 min) Write the ~150-word lead insight. Number first or early. End with the takeaway.
3. (1 min) Pick the archive post that extends it. Copy the live URL from the site (verify
   the slug, do not guess). Write the why-it-matters line.
4. (1 min) Pick the tool (check last issue; rotate). Copy the live URL. Write the usage tip.
5. (1 min) Write a fresh forward-seed line tied to the topic.
6. (1 min) Write the subject: `The Buyer View #N: <hook>`. Check length.
7. (1 min) Run voice checks: em-dash and banned-word grep, word count, no salesy CTA,
   footer line + signature present, both URLs load.
8. Save as `docs/email/newsletter/issue-NN.md`, paste into Loops as a manual campaign,
   schedule Tue-Thu, first week of month.
