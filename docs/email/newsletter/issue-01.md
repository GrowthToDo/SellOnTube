Gautham here. This is The Buyer View: one lesson a month on getting customers from YouTube, written for B2B founders. Numbers, not hype. Short enough to read with your coffee.

First lesson: your videos rank in two search engines, and most companies only count one.

Bulk Mockup, a Photoshop plugin company, says 22% of their YouTube views come from Google search, not YouTube. And Arvow, an AI writing SaaS, has a single tutorial pulling 65% of its views from YouTube search, another 20% from Google, and it now shows up inside AI answers too.

That means a buyer-intent video competes twice. Once in YouTube search, once on Google's first page, often above the blog posts fighting for the same keyword. A blog post ranks in one place. Make it a video and it can rank in both.

Decision rule: before writing your next blog post, search the keyword. If Google shows videos for it, make the video.
