# LinkedIn Writing Guideline (canonical)

**Status:** Canonical source of truth for HOW to write a SellonTube LinkedIn post.
**Consumed by:** `agents/09-linkedin-writer.md` (follow verbatim). The design/system lives in `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-17-linkedin-brand-presence-engine-design.md`; this doc owns the writing craft only.
**Reverse-engineered from** the sample-review session (2026-07-18): 5 samples + gold/anti pairs, iterated line by line.

---

## 0. The one goal

Speak to **one** B2B buyer (founder / marketer / service operator evaluating YouTube for customer acquisition) like a **friend over coffee**, and leave them able to **do one specific thing that gets them customers via YouTube**.

Two words override everything else: **clarity first.** People have no time. Deliver the value fast, with enough curiosity that they want to read on. Cleverness, wit, and structure all serve clarity, never the reverse.

---

## 1. The production pipeline (walk top to bottom, every post)

```
1. THESIS      — write it before anything else (§2)
2. VALIDATE    — 4 gates; fix the thesis if any fail (§2)
3. STRUCTURE   — the thesis picks the form, from the menu (§3)
4. DRAFT       — write it in that structure, in voice (§4–§6)
5. HOOK CHECK  — does the hook carry the thesis PAYOFF, not just the mechanism? (§4)
6. SELF-CRITIQUE — can the reader now DO the thesis? clear, on-axis, no fluff, no fabrication? (§7)
7. HUMANIZER   — kill the AI tells (§9)
8. ASSERT      — length, em-dashes, link rule, hashtags, dedup (§8, and the spec's mechanical assert)
```

A post that can't pass 6 is dropped, not padded (ceiling-not-quota).

---

## 2. Thesis first (the spine)

Before writing a word, state the thesis in one sentence:

> **"After this post, [one reader] can [execute X] to [get more leads/clients/customers]."**

X is ONE specific insight, tactic, or technique.

**Validate against 4 gates. Any fail → rewrite the thesis, don't draft:**
1. **Specific** — one narrow thing, not "grow on YouTube."
2. **Executable** — a verb the reader can act on this week ("mine your sales calls," not "understand why video matters"). Learn/understand serve execute; they don't replace it.
3. **Acquisition-tied** — leads/clients/customers, never views/subs/watch-time as the end.
4. **Non-obvious** — a knowledgeable marketer still learns something.

Good thesis: *"After this post, a founder can mine their last 10 sales calls for their next 10 video topics."*
Bad thesis: *"After this post, the reader understands the importance of YouTube SEO."* (not executable, not specific, obvious)

The thesis is also the post's success criterion: the whole point is that the reader can *do X* by the end.

---

## 3. Structure menu (the thesis picks the form)

The archetype is chosen to deliver the thesis, NOT assigned by weekday. Weekday rotation is only a **soft variety guard** (don't ship 5 of the same structure in a week; spread the 2 link + 3 no-link mix across Mon-Fri).

| Structure | Use when the thesis is… | Shape |
|---|---|---|
| **How-to steps** | an executable process | clarity-first hook → numbered steps → payoff |
| **Misconception-first (Veritasium)** | overturning a wrong belief | myth stated → one beat of delay → reveal → fix |
| **Proof-led** | backed by real data/experience | result up front → mechanism → what it means for the reader |
| **Framework / mental model** | a new way to think | named model → its parts → the part most people skip → a self-diagnostic to apply it |
| **Myth-bust / contrarian** | a widely-held belief is wrong | the belief → why it fails → what to do instead |

**Misconception-first is ONE option, not the default.** Don't force every post into it. For this brand, lean clarity-first: state the useful thing plainly; use the misconception/curiosity-delay only when it genuinely serves the message, and never at the cost of clarity.

**A myth-bust/contrarian post needs a MECHANISM, not just a hot take.** Overturning a belief requires showing the *machinery* behind why it's wrong, a causal chain, not a bare contrary claim. Weak: "Going viral is bad for B2B." Strong: "Going viral is bad for B2B, because a viral hit teaches the algorithm your audience is 'everyone,' so it pushes your next videos to the wrong crowd for months." Without the mechanism it's a contrary opinion; with it, it's an insight.

**A framework post must give a NEW or reframed mental model + a way to APPLY it, never a textbook definition.** Explaining a known concept (the funnel, AIDA, "the 3 stages") is teaching, not insight, and it's the framework failure mode. Instead: coin a fresh lens ("every video does one of two jobs: get found, or get chosen") or reframe a known one usefully, AND hand the reader a **self-diagnostic** so they apply it to their own channel ("tag your last 10 videos found or chosen"). The diagnostic is what converts a concept into an executable takeaway.

## 4. The hook (first 1–2 lines = 80% of the outcome)

Only ~2 lines show before "see more." Rules:

1. **Two lines to the promise, no setup.** Get to the value by line 2. Cut scene-setting, throat-clearing, and mechanism-explaining from the opening ("Every video is an experiment..." explains before the reader cares).
2. **The hook must carry the thesis's PAYOFF, not just its mechanism.** If the thesis is "topics that bring customers," the hook says *customers*, not "your best video topics" (best for what?). Run the check: does line 1 name the real payoff?
3. **Clarity + a curiosity gap.** State the value clearly AND leave one open loop that pulls the read ("the list is on their channel, in plain sight" → where? how?).
4. **Lead with the surprise or the benefit — never a topic or a bare instruction.** "Don't put your CTA at the end" (instruction) and "Today, competitor analysis" (topic) both fail. "Your competitor already found the topics that bring them customers" (surprise + payoff) works.
5. **Contrarian/misconception hooks are strong but optional** (see §3). When used: state the belief, then break it.

Hook anti-patterns (never): "In today's digital landscape", "Here are 5 tips", "Let's talk about", "My thoughts on", "Game-changing", "Unlock the power of", topic announcements, hype + emoji.

---

## 5. Voice (how every line sounds)

1. **One person, coffee-chat.** Write to a single reader, like a friend across a table, never a stage. Singular "you," never crowd words (everyone/folks/teams/those-of-you). Convert "most channels do X" → "you do X."
2. **Personal founder voice ("I/me"), consistent.** Posts publish from the company page but speak in the singular first person (the founder), never corporate "we/us" as narrator. (Product "we built a tool" is fine; "we believe" narration is not.)
3. **Warm 2nd-person, not preachy.** Heavy "you" is good IF it stays peer-level and warm, balanced by the occasional "I've done this too." Preachy = lecturing down; coffee-chat = leaning in.
4. **Spoken cadence.** Short sentences. Fragments allowed. Hard rhythm breaks (long line, then a two-word punch). Read-aloud test: if it sounds like a keynote, rewrite.
5. **Contractions + numerals, always.** you're / that's / I'm / you've; "15 mins / 50 / 200 views", not "fifteen minutes / fifty".
6. **Precision on the action, quirk on the color.** Any instruction uses literal, unambiguous verbs ("open," "sort," "create") so it can't be misread. Wit lives only in the framing and asides — never on the step itself ("coughs up the phrases" muddies a step; kill it).
7. **Dry wit as seasoning, not the dish.** Humor comes from saying the true-but-unspoken thing, not forced jokes. Company page = credible-with-a-pulse, never clownish or guru-ish.
8. **Plain source attribution.** "Google's research found...", "per Ahrefs' data..." Never cutesy verbs (reckons/figures/says).

## 6. Body & close

- **Narrative frame + bulleted core.** Story/observation around a scannable middle. Numbered or → bullets ONLY where there's a genuine parallel sequence or checklist (real steps). **Bullet the scannable, never the rhythmic** — never bulletize a prose beat ("Fewer people, the right people, at the moment they're deciding").
- **Numbered action-sequences are welcome** when every step is specific, correct, ordered, non-obvious, and acquisition-tied. This is NOT a listicle. A *listicle* is generic platitudes padded to a round number ("5 tips: be consistent, use analytics") — banned. A real method with as many steps as it actually takes — encouraged.
- **Scannable structure is good; the items are what matter.** A bulleted/checkmark list is not the problem — generic or off-axis items are. Keep the scannability, make every item specific and tied to customers. Don't over-correct into walls of prose to avoid looking like a listicle; a crisp list of *real, on-axis* points reads well.
- **One concrete executable takeaway = the thesis.** The reader can screenshot one part and do it Monday.
- **One idea per post.** Don't stack subtopics.
- **Reveal pace:** clarity-first. Deliver the actionable payoff by the last third, never withheld to the final line. The Veritasium "delay one beat" is a light seasoning for misconception posts only, never a reason to be unclear.
- **Close:**
  - No-link posts → a genuine discussion question that invites self-assessment ("Which of these is your channel guilty of?"). Never engagement-bait ("comment YES").
  - Link posts → a natural page-pull ("link's in the comments"), plus a soft engagement question WHEN it fits the post (contextual, engine decides). The click stays the priority.

## 7. Evidence & honesty (non-negotiable)

- **No fabrication.** No stat, metric, case-study number, or named result appears unless it is real.
- **Allowed fact sources, and only these:**
  1. A real, published **SellonTube page** (for link posts, the linked page).
  2. The **authority-evidence bank** (real stat + real named source, verified when built).
  3. **Founder first-hand experience, anonymized** — real things Sathya saw/did, on three conditions: (a) Sathya can vouch it's true, (b) no confidential party named or identifiable ("a company I worked inside," never the name), (c) not reframed as SellonTube's own work/client/test.
- **Cited wording stays faithful to the source** (don't reword "shoppers" to "buyers" if the source said "shoppers").
- **Proof/case-study posts pair first-hand data with one corroborating authority stat** (a reader discounts a company's own numbers).
- **Proof posts must carry at least one concrete, real, specific number** (from the evidence bank or anonymized first-hand), not only qualitative proof ("a fraction of the views," "demos every week"). A real number is the punch; qualitative-only is honest but soft. Get both: the punch of a specific figure AND the honesty of a real source. If no real number exists, the post is not a proof post, pick another structure.
- **No vague self-claims.** Ban "our clients saw amazing results," "we've helped businesses grow," "the results speak for themselves." These overclaim and can't be verified. Either a specific, honest, anonymized number, or nothing.
- If nothing real fits, **name no source / cite no number** rather than invent one.

---

## 8. Links, images, hashtags

- **SellonTube links → first comment** (never body; body links cut LinkedIn reach 40-50%). UTM: `utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=brand-presence` (feeds GA4).
- **Authority-video links → post body** (to render the native preview), from the curated-video bank only. External UTM: `utm_source=sellontube&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=linkedin` (a relationship signal in their analytics, not ours).
- **Body carries no URL** except an authority-video link.
- **Images:** link posts use the source page's og-image; no-link/contrarian posts get an auto-generated branded takeaway card (Step 1.5); authority-video posts show the native preview, no card.
- **Hashtags:** 0–3, only if genuinely relevant.

---

## 9. Humanizer — AI tells to kill (mandatory pass)

Scan every draft; rewrite any hit:
- Anaphora tricolons ("Big topic. Big view count. Big screenshot.")
- Negation-pairs / balanced antithesis overuse ("You don't have a content problem. You have a topic problem.", "Not X, not Y.")
- AI vocab: leverage, delve, crucial, robust, comprehensive, unlock, game-changing, dive into, tapestry, landscape
- Copula avoidance ("serves as", "functions as" → "is")
- Inline-header / throat-clearing ("Here's the thing:", "The truth is:")
- Forced rule-of-three padded to look complete
- Generic positive closers ("success will follow", "the future is bright")
- Em/en dashes (site rule) → commas, colons, full stops
- Emoji spam, hashtag spam

**Humor calibration (hands-off insurance):** reviewed samples are the taste *ceiling*, not the target. The automated engine aims a notch BELOW max funniness — humor fails louder than substance, and unsupervised generation misfires more. Past the ceiling is a credibility tax: the reader enjoys it but stops trusting you with their pipeline.

## 10. Reference examples (match the gold, never the anti)

### GOLD (thesis-first, Monday, no-link)
> Your next 10 video topics are sitting in your sales calls. You're just not listening for them.
>
> Every time a prospect asks a question on a call, that's a video. Not a maybe, a video. A buyer telling you exactly what they needed to know before they'd trust you enough to buy.
>
> Most teams ignore that goldmine and brainstorm in a vacuum instead. Here's how to mine it, in about 30 minutes:
>
> 1. Pull your last 10 sales or demo recordings. No recordings? Start today, and have the rep jot every question for a week.
> 2. Write down every question the prospect asked. The objections, the "but does it..." moments, the "how do you compare to..." ones especially.
> 3. Group the repeats. A question in 6 of 10 calls is your next video.
> 4. Make one video per question, titled almost word for word how they asked it. Buyers search the way they speak.
>
> Do this once and you've got a month of topics that map exactly to what stops people from buying. Not clever topics. The ones that close deals.
>
> What's the one question your prospects ask on every single call?

Why it's gold: clear thesis (mine calls for 10 topics) · clarity-first hook that names the payoff · one person, "you," coffee-chat · specific numbered method · acquisition-axis · no fabricated stat · ends reframing the opening.

### ANTI (no thesis — never write this)
> YouTube is a powerful platform for businesses today. If you want to grow your brand, you need to create content consistently and show up for your audience.
> [...generic platitudes, off-axis, listicle of obvious items, "at the end of the day", emoji + hashtag spam...]

Why it fails: no thesis / no executable takeaway · topic announcement · generic ("be consistent") · off-axis (growth/engagement, not customers) · listicle of obvious items · could be posted by anyone.

## Final pre-publish checklist

- [ ] Thesis written and passes all 4 gates (specific / executable / acquisition-tied / non-obvious)?
- [ ] Does the hook carry the thesis's PAYOFF, and reach the promise within 2 lines?
- [ ] Structure chosen to fit the thesis (not forced into misconception-first)?
- [ ] Can the reader finish and actually DO X? Is it clear, no fluff, no winding?
- [ ] One concrete takeaway, acquisition-tied (customers, not views)?
- [ ] Every fact real (SellonTube page / evidence bank / anonymized first-hand)? Wording faithful?
- [ ] One person, coffee-chat, "I/me", warm 2nd-person, contractions + numerals?
- [ ] Precise verbs on the action; wit only in the color?
- [ ] Bullets only for genuine steps; no bulletized rhythm; no listicle padding?
- [ ] Link in the right place (SellonTube → comment; authority video → body), correct UTM?
- [ ] Humanizer pass done (no anaphora, AI vocab, em-dashes, antithesis overuse)?
- [ ] Close fits post type (discussion Q / page-pull + contextual Q)?
- [ ] 900–1,700 chars, 0–3 hashtags?

The objective is not information density. It is **belief change plus one executable action**, delivered with clarity, fast.
