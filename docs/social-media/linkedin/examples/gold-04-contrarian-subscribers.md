**Structure:** myth-bust, clarity-first (states the insight, light delay), first-hand-or-hypothetical only.

---

Your subscriber count is the most flattering number on your channel. It's also the most useless.

For a B2B business, chasing it can actively cost you customers.

Reach and revenue are different sports. A creator sells attention, so more is always better. You sell a fix to a specific, expensive problem, so you only need the few hundred people who have it right now. The moment your channel starts chasing subscribers, it drifts toward broad, snackable topics that pump the number and starve the pipeline.

The B2B channels quietly winning look unimpressive on paper. A few hundred subscribers. Videos with three-figure views. But every one answers a buying question, and the sales calls keep coming.

So try this: take subscriber count off the top of your report. Put "demos and sales calls that came from a video" up there instead.

If you deleted your subscriber count tomorrow, would a single decision you make actually change? If the honest answer's no, it was never really your metric.
