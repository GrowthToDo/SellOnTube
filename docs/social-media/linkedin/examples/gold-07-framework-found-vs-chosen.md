# GOLD · Thesis-first · Framework / mental model · no link

**Thesis:** After this post, a marketer can sort their videos into "get found" vs "get chosen" and fix the missing half, so viewers actually convert.
**Structure:** framework. **Study for:** a fresh named model + the part people skip + a self-diagnostic (tag your last 10 videos) that makes it executable.

---

Most B2B channels are great at getting found and terrible at getting chosen. That's why they have views and no customers.

Every video you make does one of two jobs. Only two.

Get found: it answers a question your buyer is searching. Discovery, SEO, top of funnel. This is where most channels live, because it's where the view counts are.

Get chosen: it helps a buyer who already knows the options pick you. Comparisons, objections, "how it actually works," proof. Fewer views. Far more pipeline.

Here's the trap. Getting-found videos feel productive because the numbers go up. So teams make more and more of them, and quietly starve the getting-chosen half, the half that actually closes.

A channel that only gets found is a busy top of funnel with a hole in the bottom. Traffic in, nobody out.

So do this: list your last 10 videos and tag each one "found" or "chosen." If 9 are "found," you just diagnosed why the channel isn't converting. Your next 3 are all "chosen."

Get found so buyers arrive. Get chosen so they stay. You need both, and you're probably missing the second.

Which of the two is your channel actually built for?

---

**Why it's gold:** a fresh, memorable binary model (not a textbook definition) · names the part people skip (get chosen) · gives the mechanism for the trap (found-videos feel productive → starve the closing half) · hands over a self-diagnostic (tag your last 10) so the reader applies it · clarity-first, on-axis, discussion close.
