# GOLD · Thesis-first · Mistake / anti-pattern · no link

**Thesis:** After this post, a founder will give every video one specific next step, so viewers can become leads instead of watching and vanishing.
**Structure:** mistake/anti-pattern · **Source:** Gartner (⚠ verify exact figure against the evidence bank before ship).

---

Your best video probably has no idea what it's supposed to do next. So neither does the viewer.

They watch, they nod, they get their answer, and they leave. No next step, no way to raise their hand. You taught them for free and let them walk.

Here's why it stings more than it looks. Gartner found B2B buyers spend most of their journey researching alone, and only a sliver of it talking to vendors. Which means your video often is the sales conversation. If it dead-ends, the deal dead-ends there too.

The fix costs one sentence. Give every video a single, specific next step:
- Not "check out our website." Too vague.
- A resource that matches the video: "I made a one-page checklist for this. Comment 'checklist' and I'll send it."
- Or a low-friction ask: "Want this mapped to your setup? 15 minutes, link in the description."

One next step. Specific. Low friction. Where they still are, not buried in an outro.

A video with no next step isn't lead gen. It's a public service.
