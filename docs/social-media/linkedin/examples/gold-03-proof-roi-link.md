"YouTube's great for brand awareness. Blogging is what actually drives leads."

You've said a version of this. Probably in a budget meeting, right before you cut the video line.

Years back, at a company I worked inside, we settled it with real budget instead of opinions. Twelve months, same offer, YouTube against the blog. YouTube drove 3.25x more conversions. Not 3.25x more views. Conversions. The channel with fewer eyeballs won the only race that pays.

And it's not just my one experience. Google found more than half of buyers use online video to decide what they actually buy, usually right when they're ready to spend.

One thing almost nobody measures: intent density. A 2,000-view video reaching people researching your category converts harder than a 50,000-view blog post written for a broad crowd. Fewer people, the right people, at the moment they're deciding.

So if you're about to cut YouTube because the view counts look small next to blog traffic, wait a second. You might be killing the channel that's quietly feeding your pipeline.

What I'd do this week: stop comparing the two by traffic. Compare them by how many viewers or readers were genuinely in-market for what you sell. That number tells a very different story.
