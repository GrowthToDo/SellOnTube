# SellonTube Writing Style Guide

When in doubt, read it out loud. If it sounds like something no one would say in a real conversation, rewrite it.

---

## Fix #1: No Em-Dashes

Never use —. Use periods, commas, or colons instead. If a sentence needs an em-dash, break it into two sentences.

---

## Fix #2: No AI Transition Patterns

Ban entirely: "Moreover," "Furthermore," "Additionally," "It's worth noting that," "Interestingly," "It's important to understand that," "This is particularly relevant because," "Let's break this down."

Allowed once per post maximum: "That said," / "Here's the thing:"

**The test:** If removing the opener doesn't change the meaning, remove it. "Additionally, optimize your thumbnails" → "Optimize your thumbnails."

---

## Fix #3: No AI Sentence Structures

Ban:
- "Not only X, but also Y"
- "Whether you're X or Y, this Z..."
- Triple-adjective stacks ("comprehensive, actionable, and practical" — one adjective max, two if necessary)
- "Let's dive in" / "Without further ado"
- Summary paragraphs that restate what was just said

---

## Fix #4: Vary Paragraph Rhythm

- Mix lengths deliberately — follow a 3-sentence paragraph with a 1-sentence paragraph
- Use 2-3 one-sentence paragraphs per post for emphasis
- Never 4+ consecutive paragraphs of the same length
- Vary sentence openings — if three sentences open with the same word, rewrite two

---

## Fix #5: Headings Must Have Punch + Keyword

Ban: "Understanding X," "The Importance of X," "Key Considerations for X," "Exploring X," "A Comprehensive Guide to X"

Every H2/H3 must state a clear benefit, ask a question the reader actually has, or make a specific claim.

| Bad | Better |
|-----|--------|
| Understanding YouTube SEO for Shopify | Why Your Shopify YouTube Channel Isn't Driving Sales |
| The Importance of Thumbnail Optimization | Your Thumbnail Is Losing You 60% of Potential Clicks |
| Key Considerations for Email Marketing | 3 Klaviyo Sequences That Actually Recover Abandoned Carts |

**Rule:** Write the punchy version first, then slot in the keyword. A longer heading that achieves both beats a short heading that achieves only one. Single word additions are often enough — "YouTube," "Marketing," or "Business" in the right place.

---

## Fix #6: Kill Empty Conclusions

Never use as a heading: "Conclusion," "Final Thoughts," "Wrapping Up," "In Summary."

Never: restate main points in bullets at the end, close with vague motivation ("The time to start is now").

End with: a specific next action, a sharp provocative thought, or the book-a-call CTA. If a SellonTube tool is relevant, a tool CTA can appear once mid-body — the final line still closes with book-a-call.

---

## Fix #7: Specific Examples Only

Every example needs at least 2 of: a specific number, a specific business type, a specific outcome.

**Vague:** "Many businesses have seen great results using YouTube for lead generation."
**Specific:** "A Shopify app we worked with generated 1,257 conversions from YouTube in 12 months, compared to 411 from their blog in the same period."

Never invent statistics. If you don't have a real number, use "in our experience" or "we've seen this pattern." A post with honest observations is more trustworthy than impressive-sounding stats from nowhere.

---

## Fix #8: Control Bold and Bullet Overuse

**Bold:** Max 3-4 bolded phrases per 1,000 words. Bold a key phrase within a sentence, never an entire sentence. Never bold the first sentence of every paragraph.

**Bullets:** Max 2 bulleted lists per 1,000 words. Never stack more than 5 bullets. Each bullet should be 1-2 sentences, not fragments. Never use bullets for something that reads fine as a sentence.

If the post looks like a PowerPoint at arm's length, it's overformatted.

---

## Fix #9: One Consistent Voice

Voice: experienced consultant talking to a smart business owner. Not academic. Not salesy. Not motivational speaker.

- No university-essay sentences ("It is imperative to consider the ramifications of...")
- No LinkedIn-influencer lines ("The businesses that win are the ones that take action.")
- No preachy mode ("You need to understand that..." / "You must realize...")
- Pick "we" or "I" before writing. Never switch mid-post.
- **Active voice.** "YouTube drives leads" not "Leads are driven by YouTube."

**Voice test:** Read any paragraph out loud. Does it sound like something Sathya would say to a client on a call? If not, rewrite it.

---

## Fix #10: No Filler Openings

Ban these first-sentence patterns:
- "In the world of [topic]..."
- "When it comes to [topic]..."
- "If you're a [type of person], you know that..."
- "[Topic] is one of the most important aspects of..."
- Any opening that is *about* the topic rather than *inside* the topic

The first sentence drops the reader into a problem, tension, or specific scenario. No warm-up.

**Bad:** "YouTube marketing has become one of the most effective channels for small businesses looking to grow their online presence."
**Good:** "You posted 30 videos last year. Your channel has 2,000 subscribers. Your website traffic from YouTube? Fourteen visits."

---

## Fix #11: Intro Rules

- Open with a specific, recognisable scenario — not a generic statement
- 80-120 words maximum
- Connect paragraphs with cause and effect, not topic shifts
- "Why? Because" is a strong transitional device — use it when diagnosing a problem the reader just recognised
- Close the intro with a clear promise ("we'll show you exactly how to do that"), not a vague tease
- "Leverage" is banned — use "use," "build with," "turn into"
- Never "Understand that" or "Deeply internalize" — state the reality plainly

---

## Fix #12: Sentence Structure and Tone

**Short sentences over long ones.** Break compound sentences into two. Never join ideas with "and" when a full stop hits harder.

**Fragments are allowed for emphasis.** "Not on its own." / "It doesn't work." One or two per section maximum.

**Second person throughout.** "Your channel," "you're running," not "most businesses," "companies often." Don't force "Your" onto every sentence — use it when the reader naturally owns the thing.

Bad: "Revenue from YouTube comes from three things..."
Good: "Your YouTube channel drives revenue when you..."

**Puncture assumptions, don't lecture.** State the reality plainly.

Bad: "It's important to understand that a creator strategy won't work for a business."
Good: "You're running a creator strategy on a business problem. It doesn't work."

---

## Content Structure Reference

### Opening
Use TAS (Thesis-Antithesis-Synthesis) or APP (Agree-Promise-Preview). Drop the reader into a real, recognisable problem immediately — no definitions, no warm-up.

### Key Takeaways — mandatory on posts over 1,000 words
Place immediately after the intro. 5-6 bullets. Each must be a standalone insight, not a preview of what the post covers.

Good: "YouTube videos targeting 'how-to' queries outperform brand videos 3:1 for organic traffic."
Bad: "This post explains the difference between search-optimised and brand videos."

### Table of Contents — mandatory on all posts
After Key Takeaways (or after intro on posts under 1,000 words). Link to H2s only. Flat structure — no H3 links.

### Quick Answer Block
For "what is X" / "how does X work" queries: 2-3 sentence direct answer immediately after H1. Competes for position zero. Only use when there's a genuinely concise answer — don't force it onto "it depends" posts.

### Bold Callout Lines
After landing a key point, pull the core insight onto its own bolded line. 2-3 per post maximum.
Example: "**The bottleneck is not production. It is keyword selection.**"

### Before/After Blocks
For mindset shifts — use this exact format, no prose inside:

**What most channels do:** [1-2 sentences]
**What actually works:** [1-2 sentences]

### ✅/❌ Decision Blocks
For genuine either/or decisions:
✅ **Do this when:** [condition] — [explanation]
❌ **Skip this when:** [condition] — [explanation]

Use ✅/❌ in bullet lists to signal positive vs negative outcomes. Use plain bullets for neutral observations. Never mix them in the same list unless the contrast is the point.

**When to break prose into bullets:** If a paragraph lists three or more items of the same type, convert to bullets. Prose is for explanation and argument. Bullets are for items of the same type.

### Numbered Step Frameworks
For processes: H3 numbered steps, not bullets. Each step gets a bolded action label + 2-4 sentences + one concrete example.

### Horizontal Rules
Use `---` between H2 sections on all posts over 800 words.

### Paragraphs
Max 3 sentences per paragraph. One-sentence paragraphs encouraged for emphasis. Never two dense paragraphs back-to-back without a callout, list, or visual break.

### Closing — never a summary
"What to do this week" — numbered list of 5-7 specific micro-actions. After the list: one final sharp sentence. Never a restatement of main points. Always close with the book-a-call CTA.

### Comparison Tables
Column headers must be independently meaningful — "Step | Description" is a list in disguise, not a table.

Use for: Phase/Action/Why, Tool/Best for/Limitation, Industry/Problem/Solution, Role/Approach/Reasoning.

### "How to Choose" Section
Any comparison or roundup post needs a dedicated "How to Choose [X]" H2. Structure as a table or ✅/❌ blocks. Captures high-intent "which is right for me" queries most posts miss.

### Internal Links
Format: `> **Read more:** [Keyword-rich title](/blog/slug)`

- Max 3-4 "Read more:" links per post
- 1-2 internal links per major H2 section
- Valid targets: blog posts, pSEO pages (`/youtube-for/`, `/youtube-vs/`), tools (`/youtube-roi-calculator`, `/tools/youtube-topic-evaluator`)
- Never force a link. If a reader wouldn't naturally click it at that exact moment, skip it.

### Sources
Any external stat must appear in a "Sources" section after the FAQ. If you can't cite it, remove it or attribute honestly ("in our experience").

### Listicle Items — consistent structure
Every item in a "best X" or "top N" post must use the same H3 sub-structure throughout:
- **Best for:** [specific use case]
- **Key advantage:** [what makes it stand out]
- **Key limitation:** [honest tradeoff]
- **Verdict:** [one sentence]

### "Best Starting Point" Callout
One per post maximum. Only when there's a genuinely clear answer.
> **Best starting point:** [item] — [one sentence explaining why this one first]

### Bucket Brigades
Use 4-5 per post at natural attention dips:
- "Here's the thing:"
- "But there's a catch."
- "So what does this actually mean for your business?"
- "Now, you might be thinking..."

### Loss Aversion
At least one moment per post should make inaction feel costly, not just action feel attractive. Example: "Every week you're not on YouTube, a competitor with worse expertise is ranking for the exact queries your prospects are typing."

### Images, Diagrams, and Screenshots

Visual content must help the reader understand something the text alone can't explain as clearly. Never add visuals for decoration or to make a post look more complete.

**When to use each type:**
- **Diagrams** — for processes, frameworks, decision flows, or before/after comparisons with multiple steps. If the concept involves sequence or relationship, a diagram earns its place.
- **Simple infographics** — for summarising a stat, a comparison, or a key takeaway visually. Keep them minimal: one idea per infographic, no more than 5 data points. If it needs a legend to decode it, simplify.
- **Screenshots** — for tutorials and tool walkthroughs only. A screenshot of a UI step the reader needs to replicate, a real GSC/analytics result, or a live example. Never use a screenshot where prose or a table would do.

**Sourcing screenshots from the web:**
- Only use screenshots from credible, publicly accessible sources (Google Search Console, YouTube Studio, widely used tools)
- Add a caption crediting the source: *Source: Google Search Console*
- Never screenshot paywalled content or competitor dashboards
- Check the screenshot is current — outdated UI creates confusion and erodes trust

**SEO requirements (every image):**
- `alt` text: describe what the image shows, include the target keyword naturally if it fits. Never leave alt blank. Never use "image of" or "screenshot of" — just describe it.
- File name: keyword-rich, hyphenated. `youtube-ctr-benchmark-2025.jpg` not `image001.jpg`
- Use `loading="lazy"` on all in-body images
- Compress before uploading — target under 150kb for most images

**Placement rules:**
- Place the image immediately after the paragraph it supports, never before
- Write one sentence of context above it: what the reader is looking at and why it matters
- Max 1 image per H2 section unless it's a step-by-step tutorial
- Max 4 in-body images per post (hero image excluded)
- Never stack two images back-to-back without prose between them

**Captions:**
- Every image gets a caption — one sentence, plain text, below the image
- State what it shows and the takeaway: "YouTube search impressions growing from 280 to 4,200 in 90 days after keyword-led publishing."
- Never use a caption that just restates the image title or says "as shown above"

### YouTube Video Embeds
Embed only when the video directly demonstrates or proves the point and adds value the text can't. Max 2 per post. Place after the supporting paragraph, never as a section opener. Write one context sentence above it stating what to watch for.

```html
<div class="relative aspect-video my-8 rounded-xl overflow-hidden shadow-md">
  <iframe
    src="https://www.youtube-nocookie.com/embed/VIDEO_ID?rel=0"
    title="[Descriptive title of the video]"
    allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture"
    allowfullscreen
    loading="lazy"
    class="absolute inset-0 w-full h-full"
  ></iframe>
</div>
```

- Use `youtube-nocookie.com`, not `youtube.com`
- Add `?rel=0` to suppress unrelated suggestions
- Always include a descriptive `title` attribute
- Never use `autoplay=1`

### Timestamp Code Blocks
For posts teaching video structure or description optimization, show timestamps in a code block — readers copy-paste from code blocks, not prose.

```
0:00 Introduction
0:45 The problem with most YouTube descriptions
2:30 How to structure your chapters for search
4:15 The right way to write your first chapter
```

4-6 chapters. Labels must be specific and benefit-driven.

## SEO Mechanics (every post)

- **Keyword placement:** In H1, first 100 words, at least one H2, meta description. 3-5 uses total, naturally.
- **URL slug:** Short, keyword-rich, no dates. Example: `/youtube-seo-shopify`
- **Meta description:** Under 155 characters. Include the keyword. State what the reader will learn or solve.
- **Featured snippet:** One section written as a 40-60 word direct answer.
- **FAQ:** 3 questions from "People Also Ask" for the target keyword.
- **Related keywords:** 2-3 used naturally throughout.
- **Internal links:** 2-3 to other SellonTube posts.
