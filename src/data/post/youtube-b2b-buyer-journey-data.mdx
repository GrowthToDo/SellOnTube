You probably assume your B2B pipeline starts when a buyer types something into Google. Google's own data says otherwise.

In a 2025 study of 2,063 senior B2B decision-makers, Google and National Research Group found that **82% of buyers already have a shortlist of vendors before they start researching.** If you are not on that list, you are not in the running. The deal is decided before anyone opens a comparison spreadsheet. YouTube is one of the most effective ways to get on that shortlist.

Below are 14 data points from Google's October 2025 "B2B Buyer Journey" report, and what each one means for your video strategy.

## How Does YouTube Influence B2B Purchasing Decisions?

YouTube shows up at every stage of the B2B buyer journey, from first research to post-purchase support.

Google's 2025 study of 2,063 B2B decision-makers found that 60% of buyers who used YouTube during research watched product tutorials before making a decision. During discovery, your buyers watch short explainer videos. During consideration, they watch deep product demos and walkthroughs. At the purchase stage, they look for onboarding previews and customer testimonials. After purchasing, 44% return to YouTube to find new use cases for the product they bought.

That post-purchase number matters most for you. 88% of buyers who used YouTube after purchasing said they were likely to recommend the product. Your YouTube content does not stop generating value when the deal closes. It creates your next referral.

- 82% of B2B buyers purchased from their "Day 1 List" of vendors they already knew

- 60% of YouTube-using buyers watched tutorials on the product they were evaluating

Google's research asked 1,305 buyers who had a Day 1 List whether they ended up buying from it. **82% said yes.**

Read that again. Four out of five B2B deals go to vendors the buyer already recognized before researching. If you are competing on features during the evaluation stage, you are already too late. The deal was decided before anyone opened a comparison spreadsheet.

How do buyers build that mental shortlist? Through content they consume during their working lives. YouTube tutorials. LinkedIn posts. Podcast appearances. Conference talks. These create familiarity, and familiarity creates trust.

Here is the uncomfortable part.

If your company does not have a YouTube presence, you are invisible during the most important phase of the buying process. The phase that happens before anyone knows they are buying. (We break down the full framework in our [YouTube marketing for B2B](/blog/youtube-marketing-b2b) guide.)

Two things stand out.

First, 60% of buyers now use AI tools like ChatGPT and Gemini during their purchase process. They generate vendor lists, summarize product capabilities, and surface competitors. But AI is not the final word. **63% of those AI users then turn to Google Search to validate what the AI told them.** AI starts the conversation. Google and YouTube finish it.

Second, social platforms (which include YouTube) are used by 59% of buyers during research. B2B tech buyers are even more likely to use social touchpoints than average. YouTube is not a consumer entertainment platform your B2B company can ignore. It is a research tool your buyers already use.

### Discovery

Buyers start with short explainer videos and early thought leadership content. At this stage, they are not comparing vendors. They are framing the problem. A three-minute video that names the problem clearly gets remembered when the buying process kicks off weeks later.

### Consideration

This is where YouTube earns its weight. **Among buyers who used YouTube during research, 60% watched a tutorial on the product or service.** That is not passive viewing. That is active evaluation. They watched someone use the product and decided whether it solved their problem.

The report also notes that 63% of those same buyers visited the brand's website alongside YouTube. Video and website work together. YouTube builds conviction. The website closes the loop.

### Purchase

During the purchase decision, buyers use YouTube for onboarding previews and customer testimonials. They want to see what happens after they sign. Will the product be easy to adopt? Will their team actually use it?

### Post-Purchase

This is the sleeper stat in the report.

**44% of all surveyed buyers used YouTube post-purchase** to uncover new use cases or get more value from what they bought. Combined with Google Search, 83% of buyers stayed engaged with Google and YouTube after purchasing.

Why does post-purchase YouTube content matter so much?

Because **88% of buyers who used Google tools (Search, YouTube, Gemini) post-purchase said they were likely to recommend the product.** YouTube does not stop working after the deal closes. It turns customers into advocates. (See how to measure this in our [YouTube marketing ROI](/blog/youtube-marketing-roi) breakdown.)

The old B2B playbook assumed long sales cycles. Six months of nurturing. Quarterly business reviews. A slow build toward a decision.

That playbook is dead.

**77% of surveyed buyers said their buying journey took 12 weeks or less.** AI is compressing timelines even further. 84% of buyers said AI tools speed up their process.

Think about what this means for YouTube content.

You have, at best, 12 weeks from first awareness to signed deal. If your first video takes eight weeks to produce, you have already lost a quarter of that window.

This is why consistency matters more than production quality. The report explicitly says buyers prefer "short, clear, and useful content that solves problems." Not polished brand films. Not agency-produced case studies with cinematic b-roll.

A founder recording a 5-minute walkthrough on their laptop outperforms a $20,000 brand video that takes three months to approve. (We cover the full conversion path in our [YouTube lead generation](/blog/youtube-lead-generation) guide.)

## FAQ

### Does YouTube work for B2B lead generation?

Yes. Google's 2025 study of 2,063 B2B buyers shows YouTube is used at every stage of the purchase journey. 60% of buyers who used YouTube during research watched product tutorials. Post-purchase, 44% used YouTube to get more value from their purchase, and 88% of those buyers became likely to recommend the product.

### What is a Day 1 List in B2B buying?

A Day 1 List is the shortlist of vendors a B2B buyer already has in mind before starting formal research. Google's 2025 data shows 82% of buyers ultimately purchased from this pre-existing list. Building brand awareness through YouTube helps you land on that list before buyers start comparing options.

### How long does a typical B2B buying cycle take?

According to Google and NRG's 2025 research, 77% of B2B buying journeys now take 12 weeks or less. AI tools are compressing timelines further, with 84% of buyers saying AI speeds up their process. This means your content needs to be visible early and often.

### Should my B2B company invest in YouTube or paid ads first?

They serve different purposes. Paid ads capture existing demand. YouTube builds the brand familiarity that puts you on a buyer's Day 1 List before they search. Google's data shows 82% buy from that list. A YouTube presence compounds over time, while paid ads stop the moment you stop spending.
