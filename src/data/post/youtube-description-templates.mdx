Pull up any business YouTube channel right now. Click on a video. Expand the description.

Nine times out of ten you will find something like this: "In this video we talk about [topic]. Like and subscribe!"

That is 20 words. YouTube gives you 5,000 characters. You are using 0.4% of the space YouTube provides to understand what your video is about and rank it in search results.

Your youtube description is not a formality. It is SEO real estate. Google indexes it. YouTube reads it. Viewers scan it for links. And business channels waste it more consistently than any other on-page element.

This guide gives you the 3-section formula and 4 copy-paste templates for the most common B2B video types. Fill in the blanks, publish, and watch your search impressions grow.

- YouTube descriptions have 5,000 characters. Most business channels use fewer than 50. This is the biggest wasted SEO opportunity on the platform.

- Google indexes YouTube descriptions for web search results. A keyword-rich description can rank your video in Google, not just YouTube.
- Timestamps in your description create chapters, which generate multiple Google search results from a single video.

## Why YouTube Descriptions Matter More Than You Think

YouTube's algorithm needs text signals to understand video content. It cannot watch your video the way a human does. It relies on three text elements: your title, your description, and your tags.

Your title has a 100-character limit. Tags are a weak signal. That leaves the description as the primary place to tell YouTube what your video covers, who it is for, and which search queries it should rank for.

But it is not just YouTube that reads your descriptions. Google does too.

When you search a topic on Google, YouTube videos appear in the main search results, video carousels, and the "Videos" tab. Google pulls the video's title and description to decide whether it matches the query. A thin description means Google skips your video for competitors with richer text.

The data backs this up. Business channels that write descriptions over 150 words consistently outperform channels with short descriptions in search impression metrics. Not because length itself is a ranking factor, but because longer descriptions contain more keyword signals, more context, and more reasons for the algorithm to match the video to relevant queries.

## The 3-Section Description Formula

Every youtube description on your channel should follow this structure.

### Section 1: The Hook (First 2 Lines)

These are the only lines visible before the viewer clicks "Show more." They need to do three things:

1. Include your primary keyword naturally
2. Tell the viewer what they will learn
3. Include a CTA link (booking page, lead magnet, or website)

This is the most valuable real estate in your entire description. Do not waste it on "In this video we discuss..."

### Section 2: The SEO Body (Lines 3 to 20)

A 150 to 300 word summary of your video content. This is where you pack in keyword signals for YouTube and Google.

Write it as if you are explaining the video to someone who cannot watch it. Cover the main points. Use your target keyword 2 to 3 times naturally. Include related keywords and synonyms.

Add timestamps here (they become chapters). Link to relevant blog posts, tools, or resources.

### Section 3: The Boilerplate (Last 5 to 10 Lines)

A standard footer that stays the same across all your videos. Include:
- One-sentence channel description
- Website URL
- Social media links
- A brief legal disclaimer if needed for your industry

The key to comparison descriptions: be specific about your evaluation criteria. "We compare features, pricing, and ease of use" is generic. "We compare pipeline management, email automation, and reporting for teams under 20 people" tells both the algorithm and the viewer exactly what to expect.

## Description Mistakes That Kill Rankings

**Writing less than 50 words.** YouTube cannot understand your video from 10 words. Write at least 150 words. 300 is better.

**Stuffing keywords.** "YouTube description YouTube SEO YouTube tips YouTube tutorial YouTube 2026" looks like spam to both viewers and algorithms. Use your keyword 2 to 3 times naturally within real sentences.

**Skipping the first-line CTA.** The first 2 lines are all most viewers see. If your booking link is at the bottom of a 300-word description behind the "Show more" fold, almost nobody will find it.

**Using the same generic description for every video.** YouTube penalises duplicate content in descriptions. Each video needs a unique description that covers what that specific video is about.

**Forgetting timestamps.** Timestamps are free chapter markers that create extra search results in Google. There is no reason not to include them in every video over 3 minutes long.

**Linking to your homepage instead of a specific page.** "Visit our website: example.com" wastes the click. "Download the free template: example.com/template" converts the click.
