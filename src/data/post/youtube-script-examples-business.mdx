## How These YouTube Scripts Are Structured

A B2B YouTube script follows a three-part structure: a hook that states the problem in the first 10 seconds, a body that delivers value in 3-5 segments, and a CTA that bridges to a booking page or lead magnet. Business scripts prioritize search-intent topics over entertainment. The product mention belongs at the 60-70% mark, where 50-65% of viewers are still watching.

Every youtube script template in this guide uses the same skeleton:

Here's the thing:

The timings shift depending on the video format. A comparison video needs a longer body. A lead magnet pitch has a shorter body and a more developed CTA. But the order never changes. Hook, setup, body, product bridge, CTA. That sequence is what separates a business video from a talking-head ramble.

Each script below gives you the template first (the fill-in-the-blank structure) and then a complete example you can model. The examples use fictional but realistic B2B scenarios so you can see how the template translates to real spoken words.

## Script 1: How-To Explainer

The how-to explainer is the workhorse of B2B YouTube. It targets search queries your buyers are already typing, delivers step-by-step value, and positions your product as the tool that makes the process easier. In our experience, how-to videos generate the most consistent organic traffic because they match high-intent informational searches.

**Best for:** SaaS companies, consultancies, and agencies that solve a specific operational problem.

**Target length:** 5-7 minutes (750-1,000 words at 150 wpm).

### How-To Template

```
[HOOK -- 0:00 to 0:10]
"You're trying to [specific task]. You've [common failed approach].
Here's why that's not working -- and the [number]-step process that does."

[SETUP -- 0:10 to 0:40]
"Most [audience type] approach [task] by [common mistake].
The problem is [specific reason it fails].
What actually works is [preview of your method]."

[BODY -- 0:40 to 4:30]
"Step 1: [Action verb] + [specific instruction].
Here's what this looks like in practice: [concrete example].
The mistake most people make here is [common error]."

"Step 2: [Action verb] + [specific instruction].
[Example with a specific number or outcome].
Skip this step and [specific negative consequence]."

"Step 3: [Action verb] + [specific instruction].
[Example]. This is the step that [specific positive outcome]."

[PRODUCT BRIDGE -- 4:30 to 5:15]
"Now, if you want to [shortcut/automate/improve] this process,
[product name] does [specific function] so you can [benefit].
For example, [brief demonstration or scenario]."

[CTA -- 5:15 to 5:30]
"Link to [resource] is in the description.
If you want us to [specific service offer], book a call at [URL].
[One-sentence reason to act now]."
```

## Script 2: Product Comparison

Comparison videos attract buyers at the decision stage. They have already decided to buy something. They are comparing options. This is the highest-intent youtube video script format for B2B because the viewer is one decision away from a purchase.

But there's a catch.

Most comparison videos refuse to give a verdict. They list features side by side and end with "it depends on your needs." That is useless to a buyer who watched specifically to get a recommendation. Your comparison script needs a clear winner for a specific use case.

**Best for:** SaaS tools, agencies competing in a defined category, any business where buyers compare 2-3 options before deciding.

**Target length:** 7-10 minutes (1,000-1,500 words at 150 wpm).

### Comparison Template

```
[HOOK -- 0:00 to 0:15]
"[Product A] or [Product B] -- which one is right for
[specific audience]? I've used both for [timeframe/context].
By the end of this video, you'll know exactly which one
to pick for your [specific situation]."

[SETUP -- 0:15 to 0:50]
"Here's why this comparison matters for [audience]:
[Specific consequence of choosing the wrong one].
I'm going to compare them across [3-4 criteria] -- the
factors that actually matter for [audience type], not the
feature lists you've already read on their websites."

[BODY -- 0:50 to 6:00]
"Criteria 1: [Specific factor].
[Product A] handles this by [specific detail].
[Product B] handles it by [specific detail].
Winner for [audience]: [Product] -- because [reason]."

"Criteria 2: [Specific factor].
[Same structure as above]."

"Criteria 3: [Specific factor].
[Same structure as above]."

"Criteria 4: [Price/value].
[Product A] costs [amount] for [what you get].
[Product B] costs [amount] for [what you get].
For [audience], the better value is [Product] because [reason]."

[PRODUCT BRIDGE -- 6:00 to 6:45]
"Regardless of which tool you choose, the bigger question is
whether your [channel/strategy/process] is set up to get
results from it. [Your product/service] helps [audience]
[specific outcome]. For example, [brief scenario]."

[CTA -- 6:45 to 7:00]
"My full verdict: [1-2 sentences with a clear recommendation
for a specific use case]. Links to both tools are in the
description. And if you want help [specific service],
book a call at [URL]."
```

## Script 3: Case Study Walkthrough

Case study videos convert because they are proof. Not claims about what your product does. Proof of what it did for a specific business with a specific problem. The mistake most companies make with case study scripts is telling the story chronologically. "We met the client, then we did this, then that happened."

Now, you might be thinking... "But that is the natural way to tell a story."

It is. And it is also why most viewers click away within 30 seconds. They do not care about your timeline. They care about the result. Lead with the result, then explain how you got there.

**Best for:** Service businesses, agencies, consultancies, and SaaS companies with customer success stories.

**Target length:** 5-8 minutes (750-1,200 words at 150 wpm).

### Case Study Template

```
[HOOK -- 0:00 to 0:15]
"[Client type] went from [before metric] to [after metric]
in [timeframe]. Here's exactly what we changed -- and what
you can steal for your own [channel/business/strategy]."

[SETUP -- 0:15 to 0:45]
"When [client type] came to us, they had [specific problem].
They had already tried [common solution that failed].
The core issue was [root cause]. And it's the same issue
we see in [percentage or frequency] of the businesses
we audit."

[BODY -- 0:45 to 5:00]
"Change 1: [Specific action you took].
Before: [what it looked like].
After: [what changed]. The result: [measurable outcome].
If you're in a similar position, here's how to apply this:
[1-2 actionable sentences]."

"Change 2: [Specific action].
[Same structure: before, after, result, application]."

"Change 3: [Specific action].
[Same structure]."

[PRODUCT BRIDGE -- 5:00 to 5:45]
"These three changes are part of our [process/framework name].
We run this for every client during [onboarding/audit phase].
The reason it works is [brief explanation of why].
[Client type] saw [result] because [specific reason]."

[CTA -- 5:45 to 6:00]
"If you want us to run the same audit on your [channel/
business], the link to book a free 30-minute call is in
the description. We will show you exactly where your
[specific metric] is leaking."
```

## Script 4: Lead Magnet Pitch

The lead magnet pitch video has one job: get the viewer to download a resource in exchange for their email. This script format works best when the video delivers 80% of the value upfront and positions the lead magnet as the last 20% the viewer needs to implement.

So what does this actually mean for your business?

It means you cannot gate the entire value behind the download. A video that says "download my free guide to learn the 5 steps" and then refuses to teach any of the steps gets a dislike and a bounce. Teach the steps. Make them genuinely useful. Then offer the downloadable template, checklist, or spreadsheet that makes implementation faster.

Every week you publish videos without a lead magnet offer, your competitors are building email lists from the same search queries. Those lists convert into sales calls 6 to 12 months later.

**Best for:** Any business that uses email nurture sequences. Consultancies, agencies, SaaS companies with a free tier, and course creators.

**Target length:** 4-6 minutes (600-900 words at 150 wpm).

### Lead Magnet Template

```
[HOOK -- 0:00 to 0:10]
"By the end of this video, you'll know how to [specific
outcome]. And I've got a free [template/checklist/spreadsheet]
that makes this take [time] instead of [longer time]."

[SETUP -- 0:10 to 0:35]
"[Audience type] struggle with [task] because [reason].
The usual approach is [common method]. It works, but it
takes [time/effort]. I built a [resource type] that
cuts that down. But first, let me show you the process
so you understand exactly what the resource does."

[BODY -- 0:35 to 3:30]
"Step 1: [Specific instruction].
[Detailed walkthrough with example].
This is the step most people skip -- and it's why [negative
consequence]."

"Step 2: [Specific instruction].
[Walkthrough]. When you do this correctly, [positive outcome]."

"Step 3: [Specific instruction].
[Walkthrough]. This is where the free [resource] saves you
the most time. Instead of [manual process], you just
[what the resource does]."

[PRODUCT BRIDGE -- 3:30 to 4:15]
"I built this [resource] after running this process for
[number] clients. It includes [specific contents of the
resource: template fields, formulas, checklist items].
The link is in the description -- no email required /
just drop your email and it's yours instantly."

[CTA -- 4:15 to 4:30]
"Grab the [resource] at the link below. And if you want
our team to [specific service] for you, book a free
30-minute call at [URL]. We will [specific promise]."
```

## FAQ

### How long should a B2B YouTube video script be?

A B2B video script runs 750 to 1,500 words depending on the format. How-to explainers need 750 to 1,000 words for a 5 to 7 minute video. Product comparisons run longer at 1,000 to 1,500 words for 7 to 10 minutes. Write at 150 words per minute of speaking time, then trim anything that does not serve the hook, body, or CTA.

### What is the best YouTube script format for business?

The three-part structure works across all B2B video types: a hook that states the viewer's problem in the first 10 seconds, a body that delivers value in 3 to 5 segments, and a CTA that bridges to a booking page or lead magnet. Place the product mention at the 60 to 70 percent mark of the video, not the end. The how-to explainer generates the most consistent search traffic, while the comparison script converts the highest-intent viewers.

### Can I use the same YouTube script template for different industries?

Yes. The five templates in this guide are industry-agnostic structures. The hook formula, body segments, and CTA placement stay the same. You swap the topic, examples, and product mention for your niche. A SaaS company and a consulting firm use the same how-to template structure but different content inside it.
