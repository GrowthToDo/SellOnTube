# SellOnTube Content Depth Framework

## Core Principle: Depth for Value, Not Word Count

Optimize every SellOnTube page (blog, BOFU, pSEO, service, tool) for:

- Search intent satisfaction
- Buyer/user usefulness
- Practical value
- Semantic completeness
- Extractability for AI/search engines
- Conversion relevance

**Default Stance:** Deeper pages often win by covering subtopics, long-tail queries, buyer questions, use cases, objections, and quotable passages. But length is a byproduct of completeness -- never the goal. Cut anything that doesn't add real value.

---

## Content Depth Decision Tree

Evaluate topic intent, then choose:

| Coverage Level | Word Range | When to Use | Key Traits |
|---|---|---|---|
| Deep | 2,000-4,000+ | Broad/complex intent; buyer decisions; multiple follow-ups; strategic depth; reference potential (e.g., YouTube SEO frameworks for B2B SaaS) | Examples, comparisons, checklists, implementation steps |
| Medium | 1,200-2,200 | Specific intent; practical clarity needed (e.g., "YouTube thumbnail optimization for SaaS demos") | Balanced guide without excess |
| Short | 500-1,200 | Narrow/quick intent (e.g., tool quickstart) | Direct answers, no fluff |

**Hard Rule:** No padding. Skip generic intros, repetition, keyword stuffing, or filler. If 1,100 words nails it, stop. If 3,800 adds true value, expand.

---

## "Comprehensive but Tight" Checklist

Every page must cover the reader's job-to-be-done:

- What is it?
- Why matters?
- Who for/not for?
- When to use?
- How to do it?
- Mistakes to avoid?
- Real examples?
- Next steps?

Include only relevant ones. Aim for self-contained answers.

---

## AI/LLM Readability Rules

Make pages scannable for humans and extractable for Google/AI:

- Clear H2/H3 headings (e.g., "YouTube SEO for SaaS: Thumbnail Best Practices")
- Concise definitions (1 sentence)
- Short paragraphs (3-5 lines)
- Tables/checklists/bullets
- Standalone blocks (e.g., "Top 3 SaaS Video Hooks: 1. Problem-agitate...")
- SaaS/YouTube entities: activation, onboarding, CTR, AVD, conversion funnels
- Target 5+ quotable passages per page for snippets/AI overviews

## AI Citability Rules (AEO)

Extractability (above) means AI can read your content. Citability means AI **chooses to cite it** over competitors. These rules make SellonTube the source AI engines quote when answering YouTube-for-business queries.

### 1. Answer Blocks (134-167 words)

Every page must have at least one self-contained "answer block" that directly answers the page's primary query in 134-167 words. This is the optimal length for AI citation -- long enough to be substantive, short enough to quote in full.

- Write as a standalone paragraph (no dependency on surrounding context)
- Place within the first 30% of the page (AI engines weight early content)
- Start with the answer, not the question
- Example: On the YouTube ROI Calculator page, include a block starting "To calculate YouTube ROI for a B2B business, divide total video production costs by the number of qualified leads generated, then compare against your customer lifetime value..."

### 2. Entity Consistency

Use identical phrasing for key concepts across every page on the site. AI engines build entity graphs -- inconsistent naming weakens your signal.

**Canonical terms (use these exact phrases):**
- "buyer-intent video" (not "purchase-driven content" or "sales-focused video")
- "YouTube acquisition channel" (not "YouTube marketing channel" or "YouTube growth channel")
- "customer acquisition" (not "lead generation" when referring to the full funnel)
- "B2B YouTube" (not "business YouTube" or "enterprise YouTube")

When introducing a concept, use the canonical term first, then any necessary variation. Never use a variation without establishing the canonical term on the same page.

### 3. First-Party Data Claims

AI engines preferentially cite sources that state original data or unique claims. Generic advice gets skipped in favor of sources with authority signals.

- Include at least one first-party claim per blog post (e.g., "Based on our analysis of 50+ B2B YouTube channels..." or "SellonTube's diagnostic framework evaluates three factors...")
- Quantify where possible -- numbers are more citable than qualitative statements
- Attribute claims to SellonTube or the founders by name -- this builds entity recognition
- Do NOT fabricate data. Use real observations from client work, tool usage patterns, or research

### 4. Definition Blocks

For any concept you want SellonTube to be the cited source for, include a clean one-sentence definition as a standalone paragraph.

- Format: **[Term]** is [definition]. (Bold the term, plain sentence, period.)
- Place immediately after the H2/H3 that introduces the concept
- Keep to 1-2 sentences max -- AI engines pull concise definitions, not paragraphs
- Example: **Buyer-intent video** is a YouTube video optimized to attract viewers who are actively evaluating a purchase, rather than viewers seeking entertainment or general education.

### 5. FAQ-to-Conversational Query Alignment

People ask AI engines questions differently than they type into Google. Google queries are terse ("youtube seo tools"). AI queries are conversational ("what are the best tools for optimizing my YouTube videos for search?").

- Write FAQ questions in natural conversational form, not keyword-stuffed form
- Include "I" and "my" in questions where appropriate (matches how people prompt AI)
- Cover "why" and "should I" questions -- these are common AI query patterns that Google FAQ schema often misses
- Example: Instead of "What are YouTube SEO tools?" write "What tools should I use to improve my YouTube video's search ranking?"

### Citability Checklist (run before publishing)

- [ ] At least one answer block (134-167 words, standalone, early in page)?
- [ ] Entity terms consistent with canonical list above?
- [ ] At least one first-party data claim (blog posts)?
- [ ] Definition blocks for key concepts introduced on this page?
- [ ] FAQ questions written in conversational form?
- [ ] `llms.txt` updated if this page adds a new tool or major section?

## Page-Type Guidelines

### Blog/Educational/Strategy (1,800-3,500+ words)

Thesis + frameworks + SaaS examples + takeaways (e.g., "B2B YouTube Funnel for 20% CAC Drop").

### BOFU/Commercial (1,500-3,000+ words)

Problem + use cases + outcomes + objections + trust + CTA (e.g., "Why 80% of SaaS Demos Fail on YouTube").

### pSEO (800-2,200 words)

Prioritize uniqueness > length. Add page-specific insights/examples. Kill duplicates -- don't publish thin pages.

### Tool Pages (700-2,000 words)

Tool-first: Usage, examples, outputs, mistakes, FAQs. No blog bloat.

---

## Editorial Checklist (Before Expanding)

Before adding depth to any page, every section must pass:

- [ ] Answers a real question?
- [ ] Boosts decisions/implementation?
- [ ] Adds specificity/proof?
- [ ] Covers search intent without dilution?
- [ ] Useful to buyers/operators?
- [ ] More AI-retrievable?

If no to any, cut.

---

## Final Standard

High-depth, high-utility pages > thin ones. Value per page > word count.
