**SECTIONS:**

H2 #1: Why Views Don't Equal Leads (The Intent Gap)
- Creator metrics (views, watch time, subscribers) measure entertainment. Business metrics (leads, demos, sign-ups) measure intent. Your videos are optimized for the wrong metric.

H2 #2: Diagnostic #1 — Your Topics Attract Browsers, Not Buyers
- The "What is X" trap. Broad topics get views from people who will never buy. Fix: target "best X for [specific use case]" and "how to [solve specific problem] with [category]."

H2 #3: Diagnostic #2 — No Clear Next Step in Your Videos
- No CTA, or CTA is "subscribe." Fix: every video needs one specific action tied to a lead capture mechanism (free tool, calculator, guide, demo).

H2 #4: Diagnostic #3 — Your Description and Links Are Invisible
- YouTube buries links. Fix: first line of description = CTA with link. Pin a comment with the link. Use end screens pointing to landing pages, not other videos.

H2 #5: Diagnostic #4 — You're Measuring the Wrong Things
- If you're tracking views and subscribers, you'll optimize for views and subscribers. Fix: track click-through to your site, form fills, demo requests. Set up UTM parameters.

H2 #6: Diagnostic #5 — Your Channel Talks to the Wrong Audience
- You built an audience of peers, not buyers. Fix: audit your comments. If other marketers are commenting instead of your target customers, your positioning is off.

**FEATURED SNIPPET TARGET:**
YouTube views without leads usually means your videos target the wrong search intent. Broad educational topics attract curious viewers, not buyers. Fix this by targeting buyer-stage keywords (comparisons, "best for" queries, problem-specific searches), adding clear CTAs with links in the first line of every description, and tracking website clicks instead of view counts.
