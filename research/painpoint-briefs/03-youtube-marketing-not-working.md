**CONTENT ANGLE (NON-OBVIOUS INSIGHT):**
The #1 reason business YouTube fails isn't bad content, it's wrong strategy framework. Business owners apply creator playbooks (consistency, thumbnails, hooks, trends) to a business channel. Creator playbooks optimize for watch time and AdSense. Business YouTube needs to optimize for search intent and buyer questions. The diagnostic helps readers identify which creator habit is sabotaging their business results.

**SECTIONS:**

H2 #1: The Creator vs. Business YouTube Problem
- You're using a creator playbook for a business goal. Creator success = views + AdSense. Business success = leads + customers. Different goals require different strategies. Set up the framework.

H2 #2: Check #1 — Are You Targeting Keywords or Just Topics?
- "Social Media Marketing Tips" is a topic. "Best Social Media Tool for Real Estate Agents" is a keyword. Topics get random views. Keywords get buyer views. Fix: keyword research before every video.

H2 #3: Check #2 — Are You Optimizing for YouTube Search or the Algorithm?
- The algorithm serves casual viewers. Search serves intentional seekers. Business channels win through search. Fix: title = exact keyword, description = keyword variations, tags = related terms.

H2 #4: Check #3 — Does Every Video Have One Clear Business Outcome?
- If your videos end with "like and subscribe," you're optimizing for subscribers (who may never buy). Fix: every video needs one CTA tied to a business outcome (free tool, lead magnet, demo, consultation).

H2 #5: Check #4 — Are You Publishing for Your Buyers or Your Peers?
- If your comments are from other marketers and competitors, your content attracts the wrong audience. Fix: reframe topics from peer education to buyer problem-solving.

H2 #6: Check #5 — Have You Given It Enough Time with the Right Strategy?
- 6 months of wrong strategy doesn't prove YouTube doesn't work. It proves that strategy didn't work. Fix: reset with keyword-first approach, commit to 12 search-optimized videos, evaluate at 90 days.

**FEATURED SNIPPET TARGET:**
YouTube marketing fails for businesses when they apply creator strategies to business goals. The five common causes: targeting broad topics instead of buyer keywords, optimizing for the algorithm instead of search, missing clear business CTAs, attracting peers instead of customers, and not giving a search-first strategy enough time. Fix these before quitting YouTube.
