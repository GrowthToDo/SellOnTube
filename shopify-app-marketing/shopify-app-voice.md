## How it sounds (top priority)

Short declaratives. Problem-first. Plain, confident, no hype. Talks to a technical founder as a peer, not a prospect. Sentence fragments allowed when they land harder. Specific over sweeping.

## Vocab / phrases we use

- "You built the app. We handle everything else."
- "The non-engineering half of your company."
- "You stay in the code. Everything else is ours."
- "The listing is the first sale."
- "Merchants uninstall in week one."
- "You become the documentation."
- Concrete nouns: listing, knowledge base, help centre, uninstall rate, review velocity, onboarding, install curve.
- "Book a discovery call." / "No pitch deck. No follow-up sequence."

## Anti-vocab — avoid

- Agency-speak: "synergy," "holistic," "full-funnel solutions," "growth hacking," "we partner with brands to…," "leverage," "unlock," "supercharge," "10x."
- Hype/superlatives without a number behind them: "revolutionary," "game-changing," "best-in-class," "world-class."
- Vague outcome promises: "drive results," "move the needle," "take it to the next level."
- Anything implying paid-ads management (out of ICP).

## Kill-AI-patterns

- Em-dash overuse as a rhythm crutch. One per paragraph max.
- Throat-clearing openers: "In today's competitive landscape," "Now more than ever," "Let's dive in."
- Listicle padding and the "It's not just X — it's Y" construction.
- Tricolon-on-every-sentence ("faster, cleaner, smarter").
- Hedge-stacking ("can help to potentially improve").
